FAQ

  • What is Blockdozer?

    Blockdozer is a Bitcoin and Bitcoin Cash explorer. It allows you to visualize the blockchain by providing information about its components (blocks / transactions / addresses) as well as verify in a fast and reliable way the status of any transaction.

  • Why has my transaction not been completed?

    Blockdozer does not intervene in transactions, it can only show the transactions and their confirmation status. If the incomplete transaction was sent to a wallet belonging to an exchange, we recommend you contact their customer support

  • How can you help me to make my transaction?

    Blockdozer does not intervene in the processing of transactions, but you can use our service to broadcast new transactions into the network (see our API details)

  • How long does it take to complete a transaction?

    Roughly every ten minutes, a new block is created and added to the blockchain through the mining process. This block verifies and records any new transactions. The transactions are then said to have been confirmed by the Bitcoin network. Once that block is created and the new transaction is verified and included in that block, the transaction will have one confirmation.
    It is common for six confirmations to be required which takes about an hour.

  • What is the difference between a Legacy address and a CashAddr in Bitcoin Cash?

    Bitcoin Cash developers have proposed a new format for addresses to help differentiate between bitcoin and Bitcoin Cash addresses, called CashAddr. This updated format only changes how your Bitcoin Cash addresses are displayed, not the underlying private and public keys. Having a different address format for each will help you distinguish which currency you’re transacting with, allowing you to use BTC and BCH more easily.

  • What happens if I accidentally send Bitcoin to a Bitcoin Cash account or vice versa?

    Blockdozer is only a blockchain explorer and cannot help you recover these funds. We suggest you search on user forums (reddit for example is good starting point) to see what alternatives are there for your particular case. If you used an exchange for the transaction, contact their customer support service ASAP.
